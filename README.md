### ML Research Engineer — Physics-Informed Neural Networks for EV Power Systems
**University of Bristol**
*Jun 2025 — Dec 2025*

Developed ML surrogate models to replace computationally expensive LTSpice simulations for EMI prediction in wide-bandgap SiC MOSFET power electronic converters.

- Built and benchmarked five classical ML models — Ridge, SVR, Random Forest, LightGBM, XGBoost — across 518,010 rows from 6 SiC MOSFET devices
- XGBoost best classical performance: avg. test R² = 0.97, fall times and ringing frequency R² > 0.999
- Designed 13 ANN architectures from baseline MLPs to Multi-Head Attention ANNs — final model R² = 0.92 seen, 0.65 unseen device
- Applied SHAP explainability to validate physical interpretability of learned model behaviour
- **Lead author: accepted at IPEC-ECCE Asia 2026, Nagasaki, Japan · IEEJ**
